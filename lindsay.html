Meet Lindsay and Ava. Lindsay’s taken over 6,700 ClusterTruck deliveries; many with her daughter navigating from a car seat in the back. One of the benefits of a curbside delivery model is the freedom and flexibility it gives working parents.
“Take this morning for example. We had a pretty rough start to our morning. I didn’t HAVE to be here at 11am. I showed up at 12:30pm. That was really nice. You can make your own schedule. I love it because, you know, toddlers are pretty unpredictable.”
- Lindsay
It’s estimated that women make up only 27% of the rideshare gig economy, and 0% of those women are able to work with their child. The flexibility to make her own schedule, determine how she works, and be with Ava by never leaving her car is why ClusterTruck works for Lindsay. 

And, when the navigator wants to make a detour between runs?
“We’ll be running around, and I’ll have a delivery that goes past the Children’s Museum, and she’s like, ‘Ahhh! I want to go to the Children’s Museum!’ and I’ll take this run, hit 'Done For Now' on the app, and we’ll go to the Children’s Museum.”
- Lindsay
